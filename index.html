What is this website supposed to be? Frankly, I am still undecided. It will be mostly personal brainstorm and perhaps serve as a resume. In a sense this is an attempt to exercise my first amendment rights to free speech. Having been born in 1992 a nontrivial portion of my life has been spent consuming of social media data. I joined Facebook in February 2008 at the age of 15. Me and my contemporaries commonly called Millenials are the inaugural generation of humankind to have grown up with the internet and social media. As I age, I am increasingly less trusting FAANG companies and the data they collect. (Althought ) Any photo, thought, or comment contributed is no longer mine. I want to retain some control over my information and public perception. These large companies will lull the masses who trade their information for access to web services into a sense of security. I do not want to give them my data. I do not need their application features nor do I relish the prospect of going viral. (In all honestly I would love to go viral. This banal desire for attention is deeply embedded in my mortal ego.) I will need to better outline a roadmap for this website. What do I want to showcase? What do I want to share? The right to be free from self-incrimination. The right to be free from double jeopardy. The right to be free from excessive bail and fines. The right to be free from ex post facto laws. The right to be free from bills of attainder. The right to be free from the quartering of soldiers in private homes. The right to be free from the taking of private property for public use without just compensation. The right to be free from the imposition of religious tests for public office.

Biographical Information

I grew up in a North Jersey suburb called Glen Rock. We have a very large rock that is our namesake. I spent my entire childhood living in Glen Rock and enjoyed playing sports as a kid and doing outdoor activities with the scouts. For college I moved to Austin, Texas to attend the University of Texas. Their football team got a lot worse upon my arrival but I still had fun in college. I joined a fraternity where I made many friends and graduated with a degree in economics.

After a brief stint working in Houston I returned home to Glen Rock and began working in Manhattan on the Hudson Yards Project. Financial Planning and Analysis was interesting at first but got boring. I decided to leave my job and travel through Europe and Southeast Asia. Vietnam is a cool country. I learned a lot about the soup pho. Pho Ga is chicken soup. Pho Bo is beef soup and Pho Ca is fish soup. Pho Ga may have been my first words. Anyway, the soup was delicious but I lost a considerable amount of weight there. Not much bread and cheese around. Returning to the US, I enrolled at Stevens to modernize my skills for a new occupation.
